Sabudana Khichadi

Description

Sabudana khichdi is a popular Hindu fasting food where soaked tapioca pearls are tempered with curry leaves, whole spices, potatoes and roasted peanuts. It is a popular dish from the North Indian and western Indian states.

Sabudana khichdi is most commonly eaten during Navratri, Ekadashi and other Hindu fasting days. But you can also eat it anytime for a breakfast or light dinner. Due to the high starch content in sabudana, proper preparation and cooking techniques are required. Without that the starches can clump up together to gluey, sticky and chewy texture.

My recipe will help you make the best sabudana khichdi that's non-sticky, fluffy and soft with no chewiness in the pearls. If you are new to cooking with sabudana I highly recommend reading and following my pro tips to make your dish a success.

Ingredients

  • Sabudana(Sago Pearls)
  • Peanuts
  • Chopped Potatoes
  • Chopped green chillis
  • Peanut oil
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Soak the sabudana(Sago pearls) for abour 1/2 hour.
  2. In a pan add peanut oil, let it heat and add peanuts to i it and roast them.
  3. Add the potatoes in the pan and cover the pan let the potatoes cook.
  4. Add the green chillis.
  5. Then add the sabudana and cook it for about 5-10 minutes
  6. Serve the sabudana khichadi with dahi(curd). And Enjoy.
